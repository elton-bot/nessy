# Nessy — Strategic Business & Marketing Plan (Viability Assessment)

> Purpose: decide **whether to keep building** before investing more. Written to
> stress-test the idea, not to sell it. Numbers marked *(assume)* are hypotheses
> to validate, not facts.

---

## 0. Bottom line (read this first)

**Verdict: Conditionally viable — but NOT as a generic "household organizer for
everyone."** That market is crowded, low-paying, and high-churn. Nessy becomes
viable when it leads with its one genuinely under-served, willing-to-pay wedge:

> **Households that employ domestic staff (maids/helpers/drivers/nannies) in
> staff-heavy markets — the "Estate" use case.**

That is the differentiation no major competitor owns. The family tiers (Lite/Home)
are the **funnel and the brand**, not the profit engine. The profit engine is
**Estate**.

**Recommended framing:** don't build "everything for everyone" first. Build the
**staff/estate wedge** as the spearhead, with Lite/Home as the on-ramp. Win a
beachhead, then expand outward to the mass family market.

Confidence: medium. The wedge is real; the execution and willingness-to-pay must
be validated with ~20–30 customer interviews before heavy build (see §8).

---

## 1. The strategic thesis

Two very different markets are hiding inside "Nessy":

| | Mass family organizer (Lite/Home) | Staffed-home operations (Estate) |
|---|---|---|
| Demand | "nice to have" | "I'm already doing this in a notebook / group chat" |
| Competition | Brutal (Cozi, Notion, paper, free) | Thin / fragmented |
| Willingness to pay | Low ($0–3/mo, churny) | Higher ($10–30/mo, sticky) |
| Differentiation | Weak | **Strong** |
| Role | Funnel + brand | **Monetization** |

The mistake to avoid: pouring effort into out-organizing Cozi for free-tier
families. The opportunity: be the **first credible "operating system for a home
with staff"** — a real gap, especially in markets where domestic help is normal.

## 2. Market & segments

### Geography is the unlock
Domestic help is **mainstream** (not just ultra-rich) across: the Philippines,
Indonesia, Malaysia, India, the GCC (UAE, Saudi, Qatar, Kuwait — extremely high
penetration), Singapore (~1 in 5 households has a helper), Hong Kong (~340k+
foreign domestic workers), Latin America, parts of Africa. ILO estimates *(assume)*
**75M+ domestic workers worldwide**. This is a large, real, and digitally
under-served base — and it aligns with the project's apparent home market (PH/SEA).

### The 4 user types → tiers
| User | Reality | Best tier |
|---|---|---|
| **Solo living** | 1 person, light needs | **Lite** (free) — grocery, supplies, bills reminders |
| **Small family** | couple / young family | **Lite → Home** |
| **Big / multi-gen family** | lots of coordination | **Home** |
| **Estate / staffed home** | employs 1–10+ staff | **Estate** (premium) |

"Cater to all" is fine as the **vision**, but GTM must pick **one beachhead** or it
resonates with no one. Recommended beachhead: **affluent + staffed households in
PH/SEA (then GCC)** — highest pain, highest willingness to pay, clearest "no good
tool exists today."

### Sizing (illustrative, validate)
- TAM: global home-management + domestic-staff households — tens of millions.
- SAM *(assume)*: staffed/affluent households in PH + SG + GCC ≈ low-millions of households.
- SOM (Year 1, realistic) *(assume)*: 1,000–5,000 paying households if the wedge
  lands. Estate ARPU does the heavy lifting.

## 3. Competitive landscape & the gap

| Category | Examples | Why Nessy isn't them |
|---|---|---|
| Family organizers | Cozi, FamilyWall, Maple, OurHome | Calendar/chore focus; no inventory ERP, **no staff ops** |
| Chores/cleaning | Tody, Sweepy, Nipto, Homey | Single-purpose; no household-wide system |
| Home inventory | Sortly, Encircle, Memento | Inventory only; not a daily-use family system |
| Grocery | AnyList, Bring! | One module |
| Home maintenance | HomeZada, Centriq, Oply | Maintenance/records only |
| Nanny/staff payroll | Poppins, HomeWork Solutions (US) | Payroll only, US-centric, **no household ops**, ignores SEA/GCC |

**The gap Nessy fills:** a single mobile home-ERP that *also* runs the staff —
attendance, task assignment with proof, leave, payroll — for the markets where
domestic help is normal. **No incumbent combines household ERP + staff ops for
this geography.** That's the defensible wedge.

Moats to build over time: (1) the staff-ops workflow depth, (2) data/habit
lock-in (your whole home lives here), (3) two-sided usage (owner + staff both in
the app daily = high retention), (4) local market fit (language, pay cycles,
helper norms).

## 4. Business model & pricing (3 tiers)

| | **Nessy Lite** | **Nessy Home** | **Nessy Estate** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Price *(assume)* | Free | ~$3–5/mo or ~$30–40/yr | ~$12–25/mo (or +per-staff seat) |
| Who | solo / trial | families | staffed homes |
| Core ERP | limited (caps) | full | full |
| Members | 1–2 | unlimited | unlimited |
| **Staff module** | — | — | ✅ (logins, attendance, verify, leave, payroll) |
| AI assistant | — | basic | advanced |

Pricing logic: per-**household** (not per-seat) for Home to reduce friction;
Estate can add a small **per-staff** component (it scales with their willingness
and your value delivered). Annual plans to fight churn. Localize price points by
market (GCC tolerates higher than PH).

Revenue mix expectation: **Estate = majority of revenue** despite being a minority
of users. Lite/Home convert a thin %; Estate converts a fat % because the pain is
acute and there's no alternative.

## 5. Unit economics (assumptions to validate)

Rough indie/lean model — *all values are hypotheses to test:*
- Estate ARPU *(assume)* ~$18/mo → ~$216/yr.
- If CAC for Estate *(assume)* ~$30–80 via targeted/organic + referral, LTV:CAC
  is healthy at even 12–18 month retention (sticky because staff use it daily).
- Home ARPU ~$36/yr, thinner margin, mostly a **funnel** to Estate and brand.
- Lite = $0, cost center, justified only by conversion + word-of-mouth.

**What must be true for viability:**
1. Estate households will pay ≥$10/mo (validate in interviews).
2. Retention > 12 months (two-sided daily usage supports this).
3. CAC stays low via organic/referral/community, not paid ads (see §6).
4. You can reach staffed households efficiently in a focused geography.

If (1) or (2) fail, the model is a low-margin family-app grind — **not worth it.**

## 6. Go-to-market & marketing plan

Positioning line: **"Run your home like it's run for you."** For staffed homes:
**"Your household and your help, finally in one place."**

### Channel strategy (you will be outspent — go grassroots, not paid-ads-first)
- **Beachhead community first.** Pick one city/segment (e.g., Metro Manila
  affluent villages / expat + local staffed homes; later Dubai/Singapore). Win it
  deeply before widening.
- **Organic + word-of-mouth engine:** referral built into the app (invite family
  + staff; both onboard = viral coefficient). Staffed-home owners talk to each
  other (neighborhood/village groups, parent groups, expat forums).
- **Content/SEO + AEO:** "how to manage a household helper," payroll/leave
  templates, attendance trackers, kasambahay/helper rights & schedules — capture
  high-intent search; become the reference. Free templates as lead magnets → app.
- **Local partnerships:** helper agencies, villa/estate managers, property
  developers, expat relocation services — they touch exactly the Estate buyer.
- **Founder-led, niche-down:** Estate buyers respond to trust and specificity, not
  broad ads. Demo to a few influential households; let them evangelize.
- **Paid ads: later and narrow.** Only after organic proves the message and CAC.
  Generic app-install ads for a household organizer will burn money.

### Funnel
Lite (free, viral, low-friction) → Home (families upgrade for full ERP) →
**Estate (the money)** when they have staff. Reverse path too: Estate buyers come
straight for staff management and never touch Lite.

### Phased marketing
1. **Validate (now):** 20–30 interviews with staffed households; landing page +
   waitlist; measure willingness to pay. *(No more build until this passes.)*
2. **Beachhead launch:** one city, Estate-first, founder-led + community + referral.
3. **Expand:** add adjacent geographies (GCC/SG), scale content/SEO, agency partnerships.
4. **Broaden to mass family** (Lite/Home) once Estate revenue funds it.

## 7. Risks & honest red flags

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Household apps = low retention, low pay | **High** | Lead with Estate (sticky, two-sided); don't bet on free-family monetization |
| "Everything app" too broad → adopts nothing | **High** | Ship the wedge deep first; resist building all 12 modules at once |
| Willingness-to-pay unproven | **High** | Validate before more build (§8) |
| Staff onboarding friction (low-tech users, shared phones) | Med | Simple staff shell, SMS/WhatsApp login, offline-tolerant |
| Domestic-labor regulation / sensitivity (helper rights, data) | Med | Privacy by design (staff never see finances); position as fair, not surveillance |
| Solo/indie build capacity for full ERP | Med | Phase hard; the core is already built |
| Incumbents add staff features | Low/Med | They're geography- and focus-mismatched; move fast on local fit |

The **biggest red flag**: building more before proving anyone will pay for Estate.
Everything technical is ahead of the evidence right now.

---

## 8. De-risking: what to validate BEFORE more building

1. **20–30 problem interviews** with staffed-household owners: How do they manage
   staff today? Would they pay? How much? Which feature is the magnet
   (attendance? payroll? task proof?).
2. **Landing page + waitlist** (Lite/Home/Estate pricing shown) → measure
   click-to-waitlist and "notify me for Estate" intent.
3. **Concierge MVP:** run 3–5 real households on the current prototype + a
   spreadsheet for staff; see what they actually use and pay for.
4. **Price test:** present $10 vs $20 vs $30 Estate; find the wall.

Green-light to build Estate fully **only if** ≥1/3 of interviewed staffed
households express clear pay-intent at ≥$10/mo.

---

## 9. Verdict & recommended next 30/60/90

- **Is it viable?** *Yes — conditionally,* as a staff-ops-led home ERP for
  domestic-help markets, with family tiers as the funnel. *Not* viable as another
  free family organizer chasing Cozi.
- **30 days:** customer interviews + landing/waitlist; do NOT expand the build yet.
- **60 days:** if pay-intent validates, build the **Estate** layer (roles, staff,
  attendance, payroll) on the existing core; run concierge pilots.
- **90 days:** beachhead launch in one geography, Estate-first, referral + content
  engine; measure retention & CAC; only then consider paid acquisition and mass
  family rollout.

**One-line strategy:** *Win the homes that already pay people to run them — then
grow down-market to the homes that run themselves.*
